Lesson 1.4 · AI in Modern Tech Workflows

Technical Roles: The People Who Build, Maintain, and Improve AI Systems in Enterprise Environments

Most of this certification has focused on using AI tools. This article opens a different door: the career path of building and operating the AI systems themselves. For students interested in the technical side of AI, this is your career map.

The Roles That Keep AI Systems Running

There's a common misconception that "working in AI" means being a machine learning researcher. In practice, the majority of AI jobs in enterprise environments are operational roles — engineering, infrastructure, governance, and data — that keep AI systems running reliably and responsibly in production. Getting Into These Roles From Where You Are

Each of these roles is accessible from different starting points. Here are the most common paths:

💻
Software Engineer → ML Engineer or AI Engineer
The most common path. Add: ML framework experience (PyTorch/TensorFlow), experience with LLM APIs, understanding of model evaluation. Cloud ML certifications (AWS ML Specialty, Google Cloud ML Engineer) help with signal.
⚙️
DevOps/SRE → MLOps Engineer
Highly transferable. Add: model deployment patterns (model serving, feature stores, experiment tracking), ML-specific monitoring (data drift, model drift, performance degradation). MLflow, Kubeflow, or Vertex AI experience is valuable.
📊
Data Analyst → Data Engineer or ML Engineer
Add: pipeline orchestration (Airflow, Prefect), data quality and validation frameworks, feature engineering at scale, distributed computing (Spark). Python engineering skills beyond analysis scripting.
🔒
Any Tech Role → AI Governance Specialist
Fastest-growing path right now. Add: AI regulatory frameworks (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF), bias evaluation methods, AI audit processes, risk assessment frameworks. Legal or policy background is a differentiator.

The most accessible AI career pivot right now is toward MLOps and AI governance — both are undersupplied relative to demand, and both draw heavily on skills that already exist in DevOps and general tech operations backgrounds.

📌 Article Summary

What to Carry Forward

The AI roles in highest demand in enterprise environments are operational: MLOps Engineer, AI/ML Engineer, Data Engineer, and AI Governance Specialist. These roles build and maintain AI systems in production — the majority of AI career opportunity exists here, not in research.

Every major tech role has a direct path to AI-adjacent work. DevOps → MLOps is the most direct. Software engineering → ML/AI engineering adds ML frameworks and LLM APIs. Any technical role → AI governance adds regulatory and risk frameworks.

AI governance is the fastest-growing and most undersupplied AI career path right now. Organizations facing regulatory pressure (EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF) need people who understand both AI systems and risk management — a combination that's currently rare.
